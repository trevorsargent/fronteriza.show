Fronteriza vive y respira en la complex personality of borders and borderlands. Told en español y ingles,

Fronteriza is about finding your home and still having an arduous journey ahead.

Cuenta la historia de encontrar tu casa, hogar, pero todavía teniendo una travesía muy dura adelante.

It is a story of colonization, immigration, and how to heal from the deep wounds inflicted by our ancestors.

Es una crónica que nos duele porque tiene que ver con la conquista, la migración, y ese sentido que tenemos que no tiene nombre pero nos sigue.

It is a space where we can reconcile, only by bearing witness.

It is created together, with you, over time, and through many iterations.

Es creado con heridas y curas que son ancianas pero también van a durar por más tiempo que nosotras.

Sometimes this space will offer a link to listen to, or a recipe para cocinar, or a video para mirar, or a link to learn from. Sometimes it will offer you an address to meet us somewhere in our beautiful city.

Este espacio precioso es multifacético, a veces escuchas algo, o vez una enlace o te ofrecemos una dirección para juntar en este ciudad linda.

Fronteriza offers a series of events/offerings/retablos.

Inspired by the retablos of Mexico, small devotional paintings, often depicting one moment of divine intervention, as votive offerings left where tragic events have happened,

we offer our aural, visual and theatrical retablos to keep vigil, to make visible those waiting in the borderlands.

Ofrecemos estos retablos visuales, llena de alma y teatrales para recordar, lamentar, y mantener vigilia.

The offerings can be found above, all inspired by the global immigration crisis, and told through the lens of Cristi’s experience growing up along the US/MEXICO border.

So here we are. Aqui estamos.

So here I am. Welcome to my mind, mi familia, mi historia, mi vida (life). I’m examining policies and politics. And maybe I’m spilling some tea.(chisme!) Bueno, I’m learning things about myself, de mi misma, y some things are things that I don’t really want to know, (hijole) pero, I’m facing them anyway.

Thanks for joining me/us on this wild ride. Les agradecemos por dedicar su tiempo.

We hope that you will come back once, or twice, or as often as you like. (Hola, MOM!)

You’re about to get to know Cristi pretty well. La vulnerabilidad es algo muy fuerte y bonita. We should get un cafecito! Or at least correspond- enviame un email!
